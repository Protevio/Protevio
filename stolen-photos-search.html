How Your Photos Get Stolen

Photo theft is more common than most people realize. Your profile pictures, selfies, and professional headshots can be scraped from social media in seconds using automated tools. These images end up on fake dating profiles, fraudulent business websites, scam advertisements, and even identity theft schemes.

According to cybersecurity research, over 60% of images used in catfishing and romance scams come from real people's social media profiles. The victims — the people whose faces are being used — often have no idea it is happening.

Common Signs Your Photos Are Being Misused

You receive messages from strangers claiming to know you from a website or dating platform you have never used. This is often the first indication that someone has created a profile using your photos.

You find your professional headshot on competitor websites or businesses you have no connection to. Stolen professional photos are frequently used to create fake "team" pages or testimonials.

Friends or colleagues alert you that they have seen your face on an unexpected website. If this has happened to you, it is critical to investigate the full extent of the unauthorized usage.

Protevio AI helps you proactively discover these situations before they escalate, giving you the evidence and tools to take immediate action.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find out if someone is using my photos?
Upload your photo to Protevio AI. Our facial recognition engine will scan 1.4 million+ indexed faces and show you every website where your face appears. If your face is on sites you did not authorize, you have found unauthorized use.
What can I do if someone stole my photos?
Protevio provides DMCA takedown letter templates and timestamped evidence reports. You can send these to website owners, hosting providers, or social media platforms to demand removal. For serious cases, the evidence can support legal action.
Can Protevio find my photos on dating sites?
Yes, if the dating site has been indexed by our crawler. Our database covers tens of thousands of websites and is continuously expanding. We can find matching faces regardless of what platform they appear on.
Is catfishing illegal?
Laws vary by jurisdiction, but using someone else’s photos to deceive others can constitute fraud, identity theft, or harassment in many countries. The evidence reports from Protevio can support legal proceedings in these cases.
How quickly can I get photos removed?
DMCA takedown requests are typically processed within 24-72 hours by most hosting providers. Social media platforms usually respond within 1-2 weeks. Protevio provides the documentation needed to expedite these requests.
