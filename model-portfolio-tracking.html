The Model’s Image Problem

Professional models and influencers face a unique challenge: success depends on visibility, but visibility makes you a prime target for image theft. Portfolio shots, agency photos, social media content, and campaign images can end up on dozens of unauthorized websites within days of publication.

The consequences go beyond lost licensing fees. Unauthorized use on disreputable websites damages your brand and professional reputation. Agencies may question exclusivity agreements if your face appears on competitor campaigns you never shot for. And in the worst cases, your photos may be used for scams or inappropriate content.

Model & Influencer FAQ

How can models track where their photos appear?
Upload a face photo from your portfolio. Results show every indexed website where that face appears, with direct URLs and similarity scores. Set up alerts for ongoing monitoring of new publications.
Does this work for photos taken by others?
Yes. Protevio matches your face regardless of who took the photo. Whether it is your own selfie, an agency headshot, or a campaign photo — if your face is in it, we can find where it appears.
Can I verify agency placements?
After a campaign, search for the subject’s face to verify images appear only on agreed platforms. Unexpected results may indicate unauthorized distribution or sublicensing violations.
How does this help with contract enforcement?
Results provide timestamped evidence of where images appear. Compare against licensing agreements to identify breaches. PDF evidence reports and DMCA templates support formal enforcement.
Is this useful for agencies?
Yes. Agencies can monitor placements for their entire roster, verify client usage stays within contracts, and quickly identify unauthorized redistribution of campaign assets.
