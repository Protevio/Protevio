How Catfish Scams Work

Catfishing is when someone creates a fake online identity using stolen photos — often from models, influencers, or ordinary people’s social media accounts. These fake profiles are used on dating apps, social media, and messaging platforms to build trust with victims before requesting money, personal information, or intimate content.

The photos look real because they are real — they just do not belong to the person using them. Traditional reverse image search often fails because catfishers crop, filter, and mirror stolen photos to avoid detection. Protevio AI matches the actual face, not the image file, making it far more effective at exposing fakes.

Red Flags of a Catfish

They avoid video calls. The most common excuse is a broken camera, bad internet, or shyness. A real person interested in building a relationship will find a way to video chat.

Photos look too professional. If every photo looks like a modeling shoot, they may be stolen from a model or influencer’s profile. Upload one to Protevio to check.

The relationship moves unusually fast. Intense emotional connection within days, early declarations of love, and rapid escalation are classic manipulation tactics designed to build trust before the scam begins.

They ask for money or gift cards. This is the endgame of most catfish scams. No matter how compelling the story, sending money to someone you have not met in person and video-verified is extremely risky.

Upload their photo to Protevio AI. If the face appears on multiple unrelated websites, different social media accounts with different names, or stock photo sites, it is very likely a catfish. If results only show one consistent identity, the person is more likely to be real.
