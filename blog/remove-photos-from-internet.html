Tips for Successful Removal

Target hosting providers, not just websites. If a website owner ignores your DMCA request, send it to their hosting provider instead. You can find hosting info through WHOIS lookup tools. Hosts are legally obligated to respond.

Include timestamps and evidence. Protevio’s PDF reports include the exact URL, date discovered, similarity score, and screenshot. This documentation makes your request much harder to dismiss.

Follow up after 72 hours. Most hosting providers respond within 24–72 hours. If you haven’t heard back, send a follow-up referencing your original request and the legal requirement to respond.

Set up monitoring after removal. Removed content sometimes reappears. Use Protevio’s face alerts to ensure deleted images stay deleted and don’t resurface on the same or different websites.

Photo Removal Tools FAQ

Does Protevio remove my photos for me?
No. Protevio finds where your photos appear and generates the legal documents (DMCA letters, evidence reports) you need to request removal yourself. We are a search and documentation tool, not a takedown service.
How do I use the DMCA letters Protevio generates?
Send them to the website owner or their hosting provider via email. Most hosting companies have abuse@ email addresses specifically for this. Letters come pre-filled with URLs, timestamps, and legal language.
What if a website ignores my takedown request?
Send the DMCA notice to their hosting provider instead. If the hosting provider is also unresponsive, consult an intellectual property attorney. Protevio’s evidence reports support legal escalation.
How long does removal typically take?
Hosting providers typically process DMCA requests within 24–72 hours. Social media platforms respond within 1–2 weeks. Uncooperative websites may require escalation.
Can I remove my face from Protevio’s own database?
Yes. Use our opt-out page to submit your face and all matching embeddings will be permanently removed from our index.
