Why Executive Photos Get Stolen

Professional headshots of CEOs, founders, and executives are among the most frequently stolen images on the internet. Fraudsters use them to create fake LinkedIn profiles, fabricate endorsements for crypto or investment schemes, build scam company websites, and impersonate leaders in business email compromise (BEC) attacks.

A single misused executive photo can damage years of brand building. When your CEO’s face appears next to a fake cryptocurrency endorsement, the reputational damage can be severe — even if your company had nothing to do with it.

Fake Endorsement Scams

Scammers place executive photos next to fake product endorsements, especially in crypto, forex, and investment schemes. Your face, their scam, your reputation at risk.

Impersonation Profiles

Fake LinkedIn, Twitter, and social profiles using executive photos enable social engineering, phishing, and BEC attacks targeting your employees and business partners.

Executive Protection FAQ

How does executive image protection work?
Upload each executive’s headshot. Our facial recognition reveals everywhere that face appears online. Set up alerts for continuous monitoring and use built-in tools to request removal of unauthorized content.
Can I monitor multiple executives?
Yes. Paid plans support multiple searches and face alerts. Set up individual monitoring for each leadership team member and manage all alerts from one account.
What types of fraud use executive photos?
Common misuses include fake cryptocurrency endorsements, impersonation on LinkedIn and social media, fraudulent business websites listing fake team members, and social engineering attacks using executive photos for credibility.
Is this suitable for small businesses?
Absolutely. Small business owners and founders are actually easier targets because they often lack dedicated security teams. Image fraud targets businesses of all sizes.
How quickly can unauthorized content be removed?
With DMCA tools, most hosting providers process takedowns within 24–72 hours. Social media platforms typically respond within 1–2 weeks. Having legal counsel can accelerate the process further.
