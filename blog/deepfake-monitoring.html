The Growing Threat of Unauthorized Face Use

Deepfake technology has made it trivially easy to place your face into images and videos you never consented to. What once required expensive software can now be done with free apps in minutes. And once content is created, it spreads fast across the internet.

The challenge is not just that deepfakes exist — it is that you often do not know about them until the damage is done. Protevio helps close that gap by monitoring the web for any images matching your face, giving you visibility so you can identify unauthorized use early.

Important: Protevio is a face monitoring tool, not a forensic deepfake analyzer. We do not examine individual images to determine whether they are AI-generated or authentic. What we do is find every image on the indexed web that matches your face — giving you complete visibility into where your likeness appears. You review the results to determine if content is legitimate or unauthorized.

Who Should Monitor Their Face?

Women and public figures who are disproportionately targeted by non-consensual AI-generated content. If your photos are publicly accessible, you are a potential target. Deepfake creators need only 3–5 clear photos to generate convincing fakes.

Professionals and executives whose images could be used in social engineering attacks. Fake photos of executives have been used to manipulate trust and authorize fraudulent transactions.

Anyone with public social media whose profile photos could be repurposed without consent. Regular monitoring ensures you discover unauthorized use early — when removal is still straightforward.

Face Monitoring & Deepfakes FAQ

Does Protevio detect whether an image is a deepfake?
No. Protevio is a face monitoring tool, not a forensic analyzer. We find all images matching your face across the indexed web — whether authentic or AI-generated. You review results to identify unauthorized or manipulated content. This approach catches all unauthorized face use, not just deepfakes.
How does monitoring help against deepfakes?
The biggest challenge with deepfakes is not knowing they exist. Protevio gives you visibility by finding every indexed image matching your face. Regular searches and automated alerts ensure you discover unauthorized content early, when removal is practical.
What should I do if I find unauthorized content?
Document it using Protevio’s evidence report feature. Report it to the hosting platform, file a DMCA takedown, and consider reporting to law enforcement. In many jurisdictions, non-consensual deepfakes are now illegal.
How often should I check for unauthorized face use?
Run a search at least monthly and set up face alerts for continuous automated monitoring. High-risk individuals (public figures, executives) should monitor more frequently.
Are deepfakes illegal?
Laws are evolving rapidly. The EU AI Act, UK Online Safety Act, and several US state laws address non-consensual deepfake creation and distribution. Protevio’s timestamped evidence reports support legal proceedings in any jurisdiction.
