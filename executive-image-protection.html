Why Executive Photos Get Stolen

Professional headshots of CEOs, founders, and executives are among the most frequently stolen images on the internet. Fraudsters use them to create fake LinkedIn profiles, fabricate endorsements, build scam investment platforms, and impersonate leaders in business email compromise (BEC) attacks.

A single misused executive photo can damage years of brand building. When your CEO’s face appears next to a fake cryptocurrency endorsement or a fraudulent investment platform, the reputational damage can be severe — even if your company had nothing to do with it.

Fake Endorsement Scams

Scammers use executive photos next to fake product endorsements, especially in crypto, forex, and investment schemes. Your face, their scam, your reputation at risk.

Impersonation Profiles

Fake LinkedIn, Twitter, and social media profiles using your executives’ photos can be used for social engineering, phishing, and BEC attacks targeting your employees and partners.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does executive image protection work?
Upload photos of your executives to Protevio AI. Our facial recognition scans 1.4M+ indexed faces to find where those images appear online. Set up alerts for continuous monitoring and use built-in tools to request removal of unauthorized content.
Can I monitor multiple executives at once?
Yes. Paid plans support multiple searches and face alerts. You can set up individual monitoring for each member of your leadership team and manage all alerts from one account.
What types of fraud use executive photos?
Common misuses include fake cryptocurrency/investment endorsements, impersonation on LinkedIn and social media, fraudulent business websites listing fake team members, and social engineering attacks that use executive photos to build credibility.
How quickly can unauthorized content be removed?
With Protevio’s DMCA tools, most hosting providers process takedowns within 24–72 hours. Social media platforms typically respond within 1–2 weeks. Faster response times are possible when legal counsel is involved.
Is this suitable for small businesses?
Absolutely. Image fraud targets businesses of all sizes. Small business owners and founders are actually easier targets because they often lack dedicated security teams monitoring for these threats.
