2. Biometric Data Processing ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL

Special Notice: Biometric Information
Protevio processes biometric identifiers (facial recognition data). Under laws including Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA), Texas Biometric Privacy Act, and Washington biometric laws, you have specific rights regarding this data.

2.1 What Biometric Data We Collect

When you upload a photo to search, we create mathematical representations called "facial embeddings" or "facial templates." These are:

  • NOT stored permanently ‚Äì We process them and delete within specified timeframes
  • Unique numerical representations ‚Äì Mathematical vectors representing facial geometry
  • Cannot be reverse-engineered ‚Äì Cannot reconstruct original photo from embeddings
  • Used only for matching ‚Äì To compare against our indexed faces

2.2 How We Process Biometric Data

  1. Upload: You provide a photo
  2. Embedding Creation: Our AI generates a facial template (~512-dimensional vector)
  3. Comparison: Template is compared against indexed faces in our database
  4. Results: Matching faces are returned to you
  5. Deletion: Your template is deleted per retention schedule (see Section 8)

2.3 Your Biometric Data Rights

  • Informed Consent: We explicitly obtain your consent before processing biometric data
  • Purpose Limitation: Used only for facial recognition search services
  • No Sale: We never sell or profit from your biometric data
  • Retention Limits: Deleted within 30 days of account closure or 48 hours for non-users
  • Security: Encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Opt-Out: Request removal via our Opt-Out Form

2.4 Compliance with Biometric Laws

We comply with:

  • Illinois BIPA (740 ILCS 14/): 3-year retention max, written consent, no sales
  • Texas Biometric Privacy Act: Retention schedule, security requirements
  • Washington HB 1493: Consumer biometric data protection
  • GDPR Article 9: Special category data with explicit consent

5. AI Technology & Limitations

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: AI-Generated Results

Protevio uses neural networks for facial recognition. While highly accurate, the technology has limitations you should understand.

5.1 Technology Overview

  • Model Architecture: Deep convolutional neural networks (ResNet-based)
  • Training Data: Millions of diverse facial images
  • Accuracy Rate: Approximately 95% under optimal conditions
  • Processing Speed: <800ms average per search

5.2 Known Limitations

  • False Positives: ~5% of results may be incorrect matches
  • Photo Quality: Low resolution, blur, or extreme angles reduce accuracy
  • Lighting: Poor lighting or extreme shadows affect matching
  • Age Changes: Significant aging may reduce match accuracy
  • Occlusion: Sunglasses, masks, hats reduce facial area available
  • Demographics: We work to minimize bias, but accuracy may vary

5.3 What Our AI Is NOT Suitable For

üö´ Prohibited Uses:
  • Law enforcement or criminal investigations
  • Legal proceedings or identity verification
  • Employment screening or hiring decisions
  • Credit or insurance eligibility determinations
  • Any decision with legal or similarly significant effects

5.4 Bias Mitigation

We actively work to reduce bias:

  • Training on diverse, representative datasets
  • Regular audits for demographic performance disparities
  • Ongoing research into fairness improvements
  • Transparency about limitations and accuracy rates

12.1 Our Response Plan

  1. Detection & Containment (0-24h): Identify and stop the breach
  2. Assessment (24-48h): Determine scope and affected data
  3. Regulatory Notification (within 72h): Notify authorities as required by GDPR
  4. User Notification (within 72h): Email all affected users
  5. Remediation: Fix vulnerabilities, enhance security
  6. Post-Incident Review: Analyze and improve processes

12.2 What We'll Tell You

Our notification will include:

  • Nature of the breach
  • Types of data affected
  • Approximate number of affected users
  • Likely consequences
  • Measures we've taken to address it
  • Recommended actions for you to take
  • Contact information for questions

12.3 Your Action Steps

If notified of a breach:

  • Change passwords on other sites if you reused passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
  • Consider credit monitoring if payment data was affected
