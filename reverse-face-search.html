What Is a Reverse Face Search?

A reverse face search works like reverse image search, but specifically for faces. Instead of searching with keywords, you upload a photo of a face, and our AI finds matching faces across the internet. Protevio uses state-of-the-art facial recognition to compare facial features — not just image similarity — giving you far more accurate results than traditional reverse image tools.

Unlike basic image matching, our system understands facial geometry, bone structure, and unique biometric features. This means we can match a face even when the angle, lighting, expression, or image quality varies dramatically between photos.

Deep Face Matching

Reverse Face Search FAQ

How does reverse face search work?
Reverse face search uses AI to convert your uploaded photo into a mathematical representation (embedding) of facial features. This embedding is compared against our indexed database to find visual matches, regardless of angle, lighting, or image quality.
Is reverse face search legal?
Yes. Searching for your own face online is completely legal. Protevio is designed for self-search and personal identity protection. We comply with GDPR and offer opt-out for anyone who wants their face removed from our index.
How is this different from Google reverse image search?
Google matches pixel patterns and often fails with cropped, edited, or differently-lit photos. Protevio matches facial geometry using 512-dimensional embeddings — meaning it finds the same face even across completely different photos taken years apart.
Can I search for someone else’s face?
Protevio is designed for searching your own face to protect your digital identity. Our terms of service require that you only search for faces you have the right to search for.
How many websites does Protevio scan?
Our crawler continuously indexes faces from tens of thousands of websites. The index grows daily as we discover and process new sources across the open web.
